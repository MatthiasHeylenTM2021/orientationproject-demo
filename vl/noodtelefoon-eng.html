Emergency Phone

0478 81 31 28

This emergency number is very important and can provide an answer or alternative at various points in the mom's decision-making process.

During pregnancy

Why a mother is convinced that it is necessary to keep the pregnancy hidden we do not know. The fact is that she is now completely alone with her concerns and that increasing tunnel vision is depriving her of all options. It is precisely then that an anonymous conversation can bring light.

We do not judge, but try to understand whether there are any holes in her reasoning. We offer her alternatives if she remains convinced that she must give up her child. There is adoption, for example, if the mother is willing to make herself known. In some countries, such as France, a mother can give birth anonymously in a maternity ward, free of charge, and if necessary leave her child behind anonymously. In this way the mother also receives the very important perinatal care. Translated with www.DeepL.com/Translator (free version)

Lastly, there is a foundling hatch. This provides the anonymous mother with an intermediary to adopt the child through the proper channels.

So a prenatal phone call can help.

When leaving the baby

If a mother who has just given birth contacts us and reports that she is on her way to the foundling hatch, we can wait for her and welcome the baby into our arms in a more humane way. This in turn is an important moment of contact for the mom.

Since most of our children are clearly born at home, without medical assistance, we can also recommend an anonymous postnatal examination.

After leaving

Even then, the mom can still contact us. We can reassure her that the baby is OK and help ease feelings of guilt.

Should the mom regret her decision, we can weigh in with her to what extent she wants and/or can still get her baby back.

The child now has and in her reasoning. We help her to see perspective. a guardian and is housed in recognized care. If the mother is willing to step out of the anonymity we guide her to the right channels. We release her the moment she feels safe and heard.
